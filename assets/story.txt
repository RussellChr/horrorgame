PAPER LILY – Story Content
==========================

PROLOGUE
--------
The Ashwood Estate has been empty for a decade – ever since the night
that Professor Ashwood and his family vanished without explanation.
Local rumour speaks of rituals performed in the basement, of a child
seen at the windows long after the house was sealed, and of travellers
who entered and were never seen again.

You are none of these things. You are simply someone whose car broke
down on a stormy night. The house is the only shelter for miles.
The front door is unlocked.

CHAPTER ONE – A LIGHT IN THE DARK
----------------------------------
Inside, the house is cold and still. Furniture draped in white sheets
loom like ghosts. Portraits line the hallway – each depicting the same
family at different stages of time, growing more fearful with each
painting until the last, which shows them kneeling in a circle with
something at the centre that has been scratched out.

You hear a sound: a child's lullaby, coming from somewhere above.

CHAPTER TWO – LILY
-------------------
Her name is Lily. She appears no older than ten, dressed in a white
nightgown that should be cold in this weather but does not seem to
bother her. She knows your name, though you have not told her.

She says: "I have been waiting. Not for you specifically. Just –
waiting. They always send someone eventually."

She will not say who "they" are. Not yet.

CHAPTER THREE – THE DIARY
--------------------------
Professor Ashwood's diary survives in the library, water-damaged but
readable. His entries chart a descent: first curiosity about old
folklore, then obsession, then fear, then – nothing. The last entry
reads:

"The bargain is sealed. Lily will remain as keeper. The thing below
will sleep as long as a keeper remains. It fell to me to ensure
she can never leave. God forgive me."

The date: exactly ten years ago tonight.

CHAPTER FOUR – THE RITUAL ROOM
--------------------------------
The ritual room beneath the basement is a circle of carved stone,
ringed with symbols that hurt to look at directly. At the centre is a
plinth, and on the plinth is a book bound in something that is not
leather.

Lily stands in the doorway. "You understand now," she says. It is not
a question.

The book contains one instruction: speak the true name of the thing
that sleeps. But names have power. Speaking it will wake the creature –
and destroy the bargain that keeps it imprisoned.

You will have to choose what matters more: Lily's freedom, or the
continued sleep of something that should not exist.

ENDINGS
-------

ESCAPE
  You grab Lily and run. The house knows you are leaving – every door
  slams, every window screams. But you are fast and lucky and the dawn
  is breaking. The creature stirs but does not fully wake. Lily breathes
  cold air for the first time in ten years.

SACRIFICE
  You tell Lily to run while you stand before the ritual circle and
  speak half the name – enough to confuse the thing, not enough to free
  it. Lily escapes. The bargain is broken improperly and the creature
  takes you instead. A fair trade, you think, in the moment before
  thinking ends.

TRUTH
  You speak the full name. The creature wakes – and then, exposed to
  its own true nature, it dissolves. A binding breaks. Lily falls,
  then rises, then laughs for what feels like the first time. The house
  stands empty at last. Just an old house.

CORRUPTION
  You spend too long in the dark. Too long with the book. The thing
  below does not sleep; it watches, and waits, and learns your name.
  By the time Lily realises what has happened it is too late. She runs.
  You do not follow. You have a new purpose now.
